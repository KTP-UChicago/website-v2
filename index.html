Our Mission
We are the University of Chicago chapter of Kappa Theta Pi, the country’s first professional technology fraternity. We seek to build an active community of students with a shared interest in technology. Additionally, we provide our members with a forum for expanding their technical skills, professional, and social development. We seek further to foster alumni relationships and network with corporations as well as serving our vibrant local community here in Hyde Park.

Letter From the President
Welcome to KTP! On behalf of our colony, I am excited to introduce you to our fraternity.
Kappa Theta Pi is the nation’s first technology fraternity, open to anyone who is interested in tech. We are built on 5 main pillars: technical advancement, academic support, professional development, alumni connections, and social growth. From workshops, committees and projects, we offer many opportunities for members, regardless of technical ability, to learn and develop the skills necessary to succeed in today’s tech landscape. We are a culture that fosters intellectual curiosity, mentorship, and growth.
Not only do we pride ourselves with our growing network and professional opportunities, we are also a tight-knit group of technologists. With our big-little system and social events, members can bond with each other and make lifelong friendships. Even more exciting, as our fraternity grows, you have the chance to shape a dynamic culture and make an impact that transcends your 4 years here.
When our E-Board established this colony in the middle of the pandemic, it was certainly challenging but ultimately paid off. Our colony grew to 25 members of different majors, backgrounds, and interests. Outside of KTP, we are a community of RSO and student leaders, research assistants, TAs, and entrepreneurs. Professionally, we have members pursuing software engineering, product management, data science, finance, and entrepreneurship. There is no shortage of achievement here.
In KTP, we celebrate our diversity and come together with a common passion for technology. During rush, we do not discriminate against race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, or background. We encourage everyone to bring their full authentic selves to the fraternity. Whether you come for the professional and technical opportunities or looking for a fun, inclusive community on campus, we hope that you see yourself thrive here.
It is my pleasure to serve as KTP’s president, and I appreciate your interest in our fraternity. You can find more information about our rush process and brotherhood here on this website. Please don’t hesitate to reach out to any one of us on the E-Board with any questions you may have!
All the best,
Cameron Lowry
President, 2021
Pillars

Technical Advancement

KTP provides a platform for members to solidify their technical skills in various areas. We accomplish this through technical workshops led by our board and active members, collaborating on projects in different committees as well as speaker series from employers and faculty members.

Professional Development

We offer our members opportunities to take their professional careers to the next level via resume and LinkedIn workshops, technical interview preparations, mentorship and general advice for succeeding in the tech ecosystem. We are proud to have active members working in product management, data science, quantitative finance, and software engineering.

Academic Support

Our organization aims to foster a spirit of academic excellence and collaboration across the board. We encourage our members to help one another through common classes and extracurricular activities. We know we have some of the most brilliant tech minds on campus who are a terrific resource to take advantage of.

Alumni Connection

Connecting with UChicago alumni working in tech is vital to our mission. We seek to foster relationships with our alumni working at large tech giants, startups, fintech firms, investment banking and other areas.

Social Growth

Our KTP members are not just higher achievers. The students you meet will be some of your closest friends and advisors throughout college and beyond. We have a variety of social events, such as late evening bonding at the Promontory point, etc.
